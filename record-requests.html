Three core capabilities. Built for the public-records statute in your jurisdiction — Florida Sunshine, Georgia Open Records, ORS, FOIA, your municipal charter.

01  /  Intake & routing

One front door. Routed before it hits an inbox.

A public portal that any citizen can use. Topic, dates, format. The system routes to the right department before a clerk lifts a finger.

  • PortalCitizen-facing intake with record-type guidance and pre-filled templates for common requests.
  • RouteAuto-routing by topic — police, planning, finance — with manual reassignment if it lands wrong.
  • AcknowledgeAuto-acknowledgement with a request ID the requester can reference for the life of the request.

02  /  Redaction

Redact in the app. Cite the exemption.

PDF redaction with the exemption code attached to every box. The release version is generated from the working version — reviewable by your supervisor, reversible if you misclick.

  • TagExemption code per redaction. Click a box, see why. Auditable across years of releases.
  • ReviewSupervisor sign-off before release; redactions can be revised right up to publication.
  • BurnTrue burned-in redactions on release — no metadata leaks, no copy-paste under the black box.
03  /  SLA tracking & release

A clock both sides can see.

Every request shows the statutory deadline, the days remaining, and the phase. The requester sees the same clock you do — fewer "where's my request?" emails, fewer surprises at deadline.

  • ClockStatutory SLA timers tied to your jurisdiction's clock — pause for tolling, extend with notice.
  • PhaseGreen / amber / red phase indicators across the queue, sorted to the front when amber.
  • ReleaseOne-click release to the requester with download history, fee receipt, and per-redaction citation.
