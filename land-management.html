02  /  Review

Parallel review — no email-thread archaeology.

Building, planning, fire, public works each see the same package. Comments are typed against the document. Approvals are recorded against the reviewer.

  • SeeOne document viewer per record with overlay markup, sheet navigation, and review-cycle history.
  • RouteAuto-routing by application type, with manual override and reassignment in one click.
  • ResolveComment-resolution required to close. The applicant addresses each comment in-app, not by reply-all.
03  /  Inspection & Issuance

Field-first inspections. Auto-issued certificates.

Inspectors work from a phone — schedule, route, photograph, close. The certificate prints itself when the last inspection passes. The applicant gets a record-locker link they keep for the life of the property.

  • ScheduleCitizen-self-scheduling against the inspector's calendar, with same-day reschedule.
  • FieldMobile inspection app with offline mode. Photos, notes, pass/fail attach to the record.
  • IssueAuto-generated certificates with QR-verifiable IDs and a citizen-facing record locker.

Configuration

Your statute. Your rulebook. Not our defaults.

Land Management ships as a workbench, not a finished product. We configure the application packets, fee schedules, review routing, and inspection types to your code — and your clerks can keep configuring after we leave.

Application types
Match your packets exactly
Building, electrical, plumbing, mechanical, demolition, ROW, certificate-of-use — and any custom type your code defines.
Fee schedule
Configurable, versioned, auditable
Per-square-foot, flat, tiered, or formula-driven. Fee changes are versioned — old applications keep the schedule they were filed under.
Review routing
By type, value, parcel zone
A residential remodel routes one way; a coastal commercial build routes another. The clerk reconfigures it without a ticket.
Inspection types
Your checklist, your sequence
Required inspections, optional inspections, and prerequisites — modeled to match your code, not normalized into a generic flow.
